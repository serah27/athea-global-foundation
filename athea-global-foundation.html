Our Story

A foundation built on compassion and community

Founded in 2021, Athea Global Foundation emerged from a deep conviction: that mental health support should not be a luxury. As the non-profit arm of Hopewell Counseling — one of Kenya's leading workplace wellbeing firms — we bring clinical expertise directly into the communities that need it most.


From free therapy for youth to UNICEF-funded women's health programmes, everything we do is rooted in dignity, evidence, and the belief that every Kenyan deserves to feel well.

🌱
Community First
We go where the need is greatest — into counties, schools, and underserved communities across Kenya.
🔬
Evidence-Based
Every programme draws on clinical best practice, delivered by licensed counsellors and psychologists.
🌍
Pan-African Vision
We're building a model in Kenya that will scale across East Africa and the continent.
💬
Break the Stigma
Advocacy and awareness sit at the heart of our work — changing conversations, changing lives.
Our Roots
Built on a decade of clinical expertise

Founded 2021 · Nairobi, Kenya
Athea Global Foundation
Athea Global Foundation was established as the non-profit arm of Hopewell Counseling, bringing the same clinical rigour and compassion of the private sector into free community service. We believe mental health advocacy, free therapy, and community education are not charity — they are justice.

Our team of therapists, counsellors, and advocates are drawn from Hopewell's network of over 87 licensed professionals, ensuring every programme we deliver meets the highest clinical standards.

The Hopewell Connection

Athea is the non-profit arm of Hopewell Counseling — Kenya's leading workplace mental health firm. For every 10 sessions purchased by corporate clients, Hopewell funds one free therapy session through Athea for an underserved individual.

Our Programmes
What we've built
01
On The Mend
Our flagship free therapy programme. On The Mend connects young people aged 15–35 with qualified, licensed counsellors — completely free. Through Hopewell's Sema platform and in-person sessions, we've served over 200 youth who would otherwise have no access to mental health support.
Athea Core
02
County Awareness Events
We organise mental health awareness events across Kenya's counties — talks, workshops, and public dialogues that reduce stigma and connect community members to services. We've held events in 15 counties and are expanding Africa-wide.
15 Counties
03
Sema Dada
A UNICEF-funded programme creating awareness among women on mental health and sexual reproductive health rights. Sema Dada — Speak, Sister — reaches women in underserved communities, reducing stigma and connecting them to counselling.
UNICEF Funded
04
Mental Health Advocacy
Beyond direct service, Athea engages in systemic advocacy for mental health policy, funding, and rights in Kenya — working with government, civil society, and media to push for better infrastructure and destigmatisation.
Policy & Advocacy
